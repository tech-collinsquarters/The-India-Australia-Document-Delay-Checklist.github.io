01 · The Situation
Before anything else

First, don't panic — here's what's
actually happening.

Indian passport, visa and OCI services through VFS Global in Australia are currently paused. That has caused understandable worry — but the picture is more workable than it looks.

Here's what is, and is not, affected — and exactly where our team can help you move forward.

✓
Not affected

The Australian visa system.

Home Affairs is operating normally. Your Australian file can progress today.

  • Lodgements and decisions continue
  • Bridging visas remain valid
  • Health, English & forms unaffected
  • Sponsor obligations continue as usual
!
Delayed

Indian-issued documents.

Anything that must come from Indian authorities in Australia may be delayed.

  • Passport renewal & re-issue
  • PCC (Police Clearance Certificate)
  • OCI card processing
  • Attested birth & marriage records

The delay is real, but it is navigable. The applicants who move calmly and early are the ones who protect their timeline.

Act calmly, act early

Five steps to protect your
Australian visa timeline.

1

Map every Indian document your Australian application needs.

Write them down in one list — passport, PCC, birth, marriage, OCI, academic transcripts. If you don't know what's required, that's Step 2.

2

Get a lawyer-supervised review of your case.

A short review confirms exactly which documents are essential, which are optional, and which have workable alternatives (like statutory declarations or apostilled substitutes).

3

Prepare everything you can control on the Australian side.

Health exams, English tests, forms, sponsor evidence, statutory declarations, employment records — the Australian file can be lodgement-ready today.

4

Log VFS support contacts and keep any pending references.

Application numbers, receipts, tracking IDs — keep a folder. When services resume, the first-in queue moves fastest.

5

Communicate proactively with Home Affairs if a deadline is looming.

Written requests for reasonable extensions, supported by evidence of the pause, are far stronger than silence. We can draft these for you.

06 · Do & Don't · FAQ
Field-tested rules

What to do — and what
to quietly avoid.

✓ Do

  • Keep dated copies of every VFS receipt, tracking ID and communication.
  • Ask Home Affairs, in writing, for reasonable time to comply — with evidence.
  • Prepare statutory declarations for identity, relationship & residence.
  • Book health & English tests now, not when services resume.
  • Move your Australian file to “decision-ready” so you lodge the moment you can.

× Don't

  • Ignore Home Affairs deadlines — silence is treated as non-compliance.
  • Withdraw and re-apply out of panic. It usually costs time and money.
  • Rely on informal WhatsApp advice for something legally binding.
  • Send originals of any Indian document until you have certified copies.
  • Assume the pause automatically extends your Australian visa deadlines.
Frequently asked

Q.Does the VFS pause extend my Australian visa deadline?

No, not automatically. Australian deadlines remain in force. However, you can request reasonable extensions in writing — and evidence of the pause materially strengthens that request.

Q.Can I lodge my Australian application without the Indian document?

In many cases yes — with a statutory declaration explaining the delay and a commitment to provide the document when available. Whether this is safe depends on your subclass.

Q.My PCC expired during the pause. What now?

Document the situation, request an extension, and prepare all supporting evidence for a fresh PCC the moment services resume. We can help draft the extension request.

Q.Is EazyViza able to help with Indian consular matters?

No — we are Australian visa specialists. But we routinely work around Indian document gaps by structuring the Australian side of your case correctly.
